What We Do

Research

We use phylogenomics, bioinformatics, and field biology to unravel the evolutionary history of insects — with a particular focus on mayflies — while also investigating how students learn and accept evolution.

🪲
Insect Phylogenomics
Building the mayfly tree of life using molecular and morphological data to understand the deep history of insect evolution and flight origins.
🧬
Bioinformatics
Developing and testing computational methods for sequence alignment, phylogenetic inference, and genomic data analysis.
📚
Evolution Education
Studying how students accept evolution and designing evidence-based classroom interventions to improve science literacy.
Research Area 01

Insect Phylogenomics & Mayfly Evolution

The Ogden Lab has spent over two decades building well-supported phylogenetic hypotheses for basal pterygotes and Ephemeroptera (mayflies) using both molecular and morphological data. We use these trees to evaluate evolutionary processes — including the evolution of flight in insects, the development of mayfly-specific characters (subimago, gills, tusks, body form), and the ecological and genetic forces that drive these changes.

Our work spans from single-gene analyses to full phylogenomic datasets using Anchored Hybrid Enrichment (AHE) and transcriptome data, allowing us to resolve deep relationships that were previously intractable. We also conduct general insect collecting and natural history studies, including a long-running catalog of the insects of Capitol Reef National Park.

Research Area 02

Bioinformatics & Sequence Analysis

Accurate DNA sequence alignment is foundational to all of comparative biology, yet the effects of alignment error on downstream phylogenetic inference are often overlooked. Our lab has published extensively on alignment accuracy, gap treatment in parsimony, and the comparative performance of alignment algorithms — work that has shaped how the field evaluates its own methods.

Current work focuses on bioinformatic pipelines for processing Anchored Hybrid Enrichment data and transcriptomes, developing workflows for large-scale phylogenomic analyses, and characterizing opsin gene evolution across mayfly lineages using RNA-seq data.

Active Projects
AHE Pipeline Development Transcriptome Assembly Opsin Gene Characterization Alignment Accuracy Studies Concurrent Analysis Methods
Research Area 03

Biology Education & Evolution Acceptance

A substantial body of research demonstrates that many students — especially at religiously-affiliated or faith-diverse institutions — struggle to accept evolutionary theory despite understanding it. Our lab investigates what factors predict acceptance, what interventions actually change minds, and how instructional design affects both learning outcomes and attitudes.

Specific projects include longitudinal studies of evolution acceptance across the biology curriculum, comparison of flipped vs. traditional classrooms, evaluation of online vs. face-to-face instruction, and the effectiveness of hands-on hominin skull labs in improving human evolution acceptance. This work has real impact: our findings are being used to redesign introductory biology courses at UVU and inform national conversations about science education.

Active Projects
Evolution Acceptance in Biology Majors Hominin Skull Lab Interventions Online vs. Face-to-Face Biology Flipped Classroom Effectiveness Longitudinal Acceptance Studies Religion & Science Reconciliation

Methods & Techniques

The tools we use across our research programs.

🔬
Anchored Hybrid Enrichment
Target capture sequencing for phylogenomic datasets across hundreds of loci.
💻
Transcriptome Analysis
RNA-seq assembly and annotation for gene expression and evolutionary studies.
🧪
Morphological Analysis
Traditional and landmark-based morphometrics integrated with molecular data.
🗺️
Field Collecting
Insect sampling at Capitol Reef NP and across Utah's diverse habitats.
🌳
Maximum Likelihood & Bayesian
IQ-TREE, RAxML, MrBayes for phylogenetic inference at multiple scales.
📊
Sequence Alignment
MAFFT, MUSCLE, POY — plus simulation studies evaluating accuracy.
📋
Survey Research
Validated instruments (MATE, I-SEA) for measuring evolution acceptance.
🔭
Specimen Curation
Wet and dry insect collections, photographic vouchers, and DNA banking.
