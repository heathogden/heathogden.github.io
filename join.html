What We Study

Find your project

We have active projects across four areas. You don't need prior experience in any of them — just genuine interest in at least one.

🪲 Mayfly Phylogenetics & Evolution

We use DNA sequencing (including Anchored Hybrid Enrichment and transcriptomes) to build the mayfly tree of life and understand how flight, tusks, gills, and other traits evolved. Current projects span Heptageniidae, Leptophlebiidae, Baetidae, and more.

🔬 Entomology & Natural History

Field collecting, specimen identification, and insect surveys — including our ongoing catalog of the insects of Capitol Reef National Park. If you like being outside and learning to identify insects, this is for you.

💻 Bioinformatics

We build and run computational pipelines for phylogenomic data — sequence alignment, tree inference, transcriptome assembly, and opsin gene analysis. Students learn Linux, command-line tools, and phylogenetic software used by real researchers.

📊 Evolution Acceptance Research

We study how students learn and accept evolutionary theory, and we design interventions to improve science literacy. Projects include longitudinal surveys, analysis of flipped vs. traditional classrooms, and studies on the role of hominin skull labs and media in shaping student attitudes.

What we look for

We're selective — but not about GPA.

🔍
Real curiosity
You're genuinely interested in insects, evolution, bioinformatics, or science education — not just looking for a line on your résumé.
📅
Reliability
Show up when you say you will. Communicate when something comes up. Research depends on people following through.
🌱
Willingness to learn
Every student starts not knowing our methods. That's expected. We want students who dig in and ask questions.
You do NOT need: prior research experience, a high GPA, to be a biology major, or to know bioinformatics. We have trained students from scratch many times. If you're genuinely interested, apply.
