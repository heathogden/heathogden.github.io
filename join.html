Prospective Students

Do real science.
Starting now.

The Ogden Lab is built on undergraduate research. We don't treat students as lab assistants — we treat them as scientists. You'll have your own project, present at conferences, and leave with skills that open doors to graduate school and careers in science.

🏆
Conference Presentations
Our undergraduates present at national meetings — ESA, UCUR, Evolution — often in their first or second year.
📄
Published Co-Authors
Many lab alumni are published co-authors on peer-reviewed papers before they graduate.
🎓
Graduate School Pathways
Dozens of Ogden Lab alumni have gone on to PhD programs, medical school, and professional science careers.
What you gain

A genuine research experience

Most undergraduates never do real science. In the Ogden Lab, you will. From your first semester you'll be working on actual research problems — not practice problems, not busy work — in insect evolution, bioinformatics, or education research.

Dr. Ogden has mentored over 87 undergraduates and knows that the best researchers often start with zero experience. What makes the difference is curiosity, reliability, and a willingness to learn.

  • 🔬
    Your own research project
    Not pipetting for someone else. You'll own a question, collect data, and interpret results.
  • 🧬
    Bioinformatics & lab skills
    Linux, phylogenetic software, sequence analysis, specimen handling — real tools used in real science.
  • 🎤
    Conference presentations
    ESA, UCUR, UVU Showcase. You'll learn to present your work to scientific audiences.
  • 📝
    Publication opportunities
    Strong projects become papers. Several undergraduates in this lab are published co-authors.
  • 🗺️
    Field work
    Insect collecting at Capitol Reef National Park and across Utah.
  • 🤝
    Mentorship for your future
    Dr. Ogden writes strong letters of recommendation and actively helps students get into graduate programs.

What we look for

We're selective — but not in the way you might think.

🔍
Genuine curiosity
You find insects, genomes, or evolution genuinely interesting — not just as résumé items. You ask questions. You look things up because you want to know.
📅
Reliability & commitment
Research requires consistency. We look for students who show up, communicate, and follow through — not necessarily the highest GPA.
🌱
Willingness to learn
Every new student starts knowing very little about our methods. That's expected. What matters is whether you're willing to put in the work.
You do NOT need: prior research experience, a high GPA, to be a biology major, or to already know bioinformatics. We have trained students from scratch many times. If you're genuinely interested, apply.
