Prospective Students

Join the Ogden Lab

We welcome curious, motivated undergraduate and graduate students who want to do real research in insect evolution, bioinformatics, or science education.

What We're Looking For

No prior research experience is required. What matters most is curiosity about evolutionary biology or bioinformatics, and a willingness to commit time and effort to a project. Students of all backgrounds are welcome.

Available Projects

  • Phylogenomics of mayflies (Ephemeroptera) — molecular data analysis
  • Insect collecting and natural history at Capitol Reef National Park
  • Bioinformatics pipeline development and sequence analysis
  • Evolution acceptance education research — classroom-based studies
  • Opsin gene characterization using transcriptome data

What Students Gain

  • Authentic research experience — real data, real questions, real results
  • Opportunity to co-author publications and present at conferences
  • Mentorship for graduate school and career planning
  • Skills in bioinformatics, phylogenetics, field collecting, and more

How to Apply

Send Dr. Ogden an email introducing yourself. Include:

  • Your year and major
  • Why you're interested in the lab
  • Which project area interests you most
  • Your availability (hours/week)
