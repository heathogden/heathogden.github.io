Research Areas
What we study

Three pillars of inquiry

Our work sits at the intersection of evolutionary biology, bioinformatics, and education research — asking questions that range from the branching of the mayfly tree of life to how students accept evolution.

🦟

Insect Phylogenomics & Evolution

We develop well-supported phylogenetic hypotheses for basal pterygotes and Ephemeroptera using molecular and morphological data, then use these trees to evaluate evolutionary processes including the origin of flight, mayfly gills, tusks, and body form.

🧬

Bioinformatics & Sequence Analysis

We investigate sequence alignment accuracy, topology inference, indel models, and concurrent analysis methodologies — improving the computational tools that underpin all of comparative biology.

📚

Pedagogy & Evolution Education

We study flipped classrooms, online vs. face-to-face learning, and factors that influence evolution acceptance among students — and develop evidence-based classroom interventions to improve outcomes.

Ongoing Research Projects

01

Phylogenetics and Evolution of Mayflies (Ephemeroptera)

Comprehensive multi-locus and morphological study of the mayfly tree of life.

02

Phylogeny of Coloburiscidae

Resolving relationships within this understudied basal mayfly family.

03

Phylogeny of Siphlonuridae

Investigating the minnow mayflies, including novel transcriptomic data.

04

Carnivorous Mayflies (Ameletopsidae)

Resolving the evolutionary placement of this remarkable predatory lineage.

05

Opsin Genes in Mayflies

Characterizing opsin diversity using transcriptome data to understand vision evolution.

06

Insects of Capitol Reef National Park

Ongoing natural history catalog of the insect fauna of Capitol Reef NP, Utah.

07

Evolution Acceptance in Students

Studying and improving evolution acceptance in general biology and major students through evidence-based interventions including hominin skull labs.

08

Online vs. Face-to-Face Instruction

Comparative study of learning outcomes across different instructional modalities in biology courses.
